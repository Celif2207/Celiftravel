There are trips you plan for months, and then there are trips that simply grab you by the soul and never let go. Tanzania was the latter for us. We left Stuttgart on the 20th of September 2025 and came back 17 days later as completely different people. This is our honest account of everything — the magic, the costs, the mistakes and the memories we will carry for the rest of our lives.

Our route: 17 days, 12 destinations

We split the trip into two very different halves. The first week was pure wildlife — dusty jeep tracks, early mornings and the kind of silence that only exists in the middle of the African bush. The second half was all about slowing down on the beaches of Zanzibar.

Our full route
  • Day 1: Fly Stuttgart → Dar es Salaam → straight connecting flight to Arusha
  • Day 2: Arusha — check in at Serengeti Villa, safari briefing
  • Day 3: Tarangire National Park — elephants everywhere
  • Day 4: Serengeti — the Great Migration and lions at sunrise
  • Day 5: Ngorongoro Crater — Big Five in one day, Maasai village visit
  • Day 6: Back to Arusha — one more night at Serengeti Villa
  • Day 7: Bolt to Moshi, walked around town, views of Kilimanjaro, transfer to Kilimanjaro Airport → fly to Zanzibar
  • Day 8–9: Nungwi — white sand, dhow sunsets
  • Day 10: Kendwa — snorkelling, massage, beach bars
  • Day 11–13: Paje — our favourite beach, kite surfers, local restaurants
  • Day 14–15: Jambiani — quiet, authentic, the most beautiful low tide we've ever seen
  • Day 16: Stone Town — spice market, Slave Museum, Forodhani night market
  • Day 17: Fly home

"We skipped Dar es Salaam completely and flew straight to Arusha — best decision we made on day one."

The safari: Tarangire, Serengeti & Ngorongoro

How we booked — and why we'd do it the same way again

Before the trip we searched on Booking.com for accommodation in Arusha that also organises safaris — the idea being to arrive, settle in, and have everything arranged from one place. That's how we found Serengeti Villa (15096 Kijenge, Arusha). The reviews were excellent, and the concept was exactly what we were looking for: a guesthouse run by a local family who also organise customised safaris.

We contacted the owner — Anthony — directly via WhatsApp before booking — he was responsive, friendly and spoke great English. We discussed the itinerary, what was included, group size and pricing all through chat. No agency fees, no middlemen. After a few messages we decided on a 4-day safari covering Tarangire, the Serengeti and the Ngorongoro Crater. We were picked up directly from Arusha Airport on arrival — no taxi needed, no stress on day one.

How to book a safari like we did
  • Search Booking.com for guesthouses in Arusha that also organise safaris — filter by rating
  • Contact them directly via WhatsApp before booking — you'll get a much better feel for who you're dealing with
  • Ask about group size, what's included (meals, park fees, accommodation) and what's not
  • Serengeti Villa (Kijenge, Arusha) — highly recommended, airport pickup included
  • Book at least 4 days — Tarangire, Serengeti and Ngorongoro deserve proper time
  • They also have a guesthouse in Moshi — great if you want to see Kilimanjaro before flying out

After 4 incredible safari days we returned to Serengeti Villa in Arusha for one last night. The next morning we took a Bolt taxi to their sister property in Moshi — we wanted to see Kilimanjaro up close before leaving. We dropped our bags, walked around Moshi for a few hours, and in the afternoon we were transferred directly to Kilimanjaro Airport. From there we flew to Zanzibar for 277€ for two — one of the best decisions of the whole trip.

What was included — and what it cost

We chose the group camping safari at 930 USD per person (1,860 USD / ~1,581€ for two). Everything was included: all park and camping fees, full board meals (breakfast, hot lunch and dinner), a private 4WD jeep with a roof hatch and charging docks, a dedicated safari driver and cook, tents, sleeping bags, mattresses, mineral water, airport pickup and one night at Serengeti Villa before and after the safari. The only extras were tips, visa fees and travel insurance.

Day by day: what the safari looked like
  • Day 1 — Tarangire National Park: picked up from Serengeti Villa, drove into the park along the Tarangire River. Baobab trees, huge elephant herds, rare Greater Kudu. Picnic lunch, afternoon game drive. Overnight at Jua Home Campsite
  • Day 2 — Serengeti: early start, drove into the endless plains. Kopjes (giant rock formations), leopards, lions, the Great Migration. Camped overnight at Seronera Valley — in the heart of the Serengeti, surrounded by wildlife
  • Day 3 — Serengeti to Ngorongoro: second full day deep in the Serengeti, then drove toward the crater. Arrived at the rim at sunset — the view stopped us completely. Camped overnight at Simba A Campsite on the crater's edge
  • Day 4 — Ngorongoro Crater: descended into the crater at sunrise. Grasslands, fever tree forest, Lake Magadi, black rhino, lions, hippos — Big Five in one day. Picnic lunch inside the crater, then back to Arusha

Safari tips from experience
  • Book a 4-day safari minimum — 2-3 days is not enough to cover Tarangire, Serengeti and Ngorongoro properly
  • Budget around 930 USD per person for a solid group camping safari
  • Always tip your driver and cook — they make or break your experience
  • Bring Red Bull and snacks for long driving days — we paid 6€ for two Red Bulls in the Serengeti
  • September is excellent — dry season, animals gather around water sources

Zanzibar: Nungwi, Kendwa, Paje & Stone Town

We flew from Kilimanjaro Airport directly to Zanzibar — 277€ for two, 45 minutes in the air, and suddenly we were standing on white sand with turquoise water in front of us. The contrast with the dusty safari roads couldn't have been more dramatic.

We moved around Zanzibar quite a bit, which we'd recommend — each beach has its own personality. Nungwi in the north is lively and social, great for sunsets. Kendwa next door is slightly quieter, where we had a memorable 50-minute massage for two (just 53 USD total). Paje was our favourite — a genuine fishing village with kite surfers, cheap local restaurants and the best coffee of the trip at El Capitano.

Jambiani was the most authentic. Barely any tourists, fishermen pulling in their boats at low tide, and the most dramatic tidal flats we've ever walked across. We ate at Chez Hasan — grilled fish for two with drinks, 60,000 TZS (~20€). Stone Town was our final stop and the perfect ending — the narrow alleys, the carved wooden doors, the Slave Museum (10€ entry, deeply moving) and the Forodhani night market where we ate for almost nothing.

Where we ate & what we loved

Tanzanian food is underrated. The fresh seafood on Zanzibar was extraordinary — grilled tuna, calamari, octopus curry — all caught that morning, all for prices that felt almost impossible. Our favourite meal was at Out of Africa in Stone Town: 2 tuna dishes, a papaya salad and drinks for 110,000 TZS (~36€) for two. Our most affordable meal was calamari, chapati and two Fantas in Paje for just 30,000 TZS (~10€).

We drank a lot of fresh coconut water (10,000 TZS, ~3€ each), ate Zanzibar pizza at the Forodhani night market and discovered Tanzanian tea — a sweet, spiced milk tea that we had with "sweet pizza" in Paje for 26,000 TZS (~8.50€). The coffee culture was a pleasant surprise too — good espresso was available everywhere.

Our favourite spots to eat
  • Out of Africa, Stone Town — fresh tuna and the best papaya salad (~36€ for two)
  • Roots, Paje — relaxed dinners under the stars (~29€ for two)
  • Chez Hasan, Jambiani — grilled fish by the sea (~20€ for two)
  • El Capitano, Paje — best coffee on the island and great breakfast (~16€ for two)
  • Forodhani Night Market, Stone Town — street food for almost nothing

The real costs: what we spent

We tracked every single expense on this trip. Total spend for two people over 17 days: approximately 5,100€. Here's exactly where the money went:

Full budget breakdown (2 people, 17 days)
  • Flights Stuttgart ↔ Tanzania: 1,470€
  • Internal flights (Dar es Salaam → Arusha + Kilimanjaro → Zanzibar): 357€
  • 4-day safari (Tarangire, Serengeti, Ngorongoro): 1,581€
  • Accommodation Zanzibar (10 nights): ~415€
  • Food & drinks: ~423€
  • Activities (snorkelling, Maasai village, Slave Museum etc.): ~200€
  • Transport on Zanzibar (taxis & tuk tuks): ~100€
  • Visas (2 persons): ~86€
  • Travel insurance Zanzibar: ~75€
  • Shopping & souvenirs: ~120€
  • Tips: ~155€

"Per person, per day: roughly 150€ including flights. Without flights, closer to 80€ per person per day."

Practical tips & what to pack

Before you go
  • Visa: apply online at immigration.go.tz — costs ~50 USD per person
  • Travel insurance: get one that covers safari and water activities — we paid 88 USD for two
  • SIM card: buy on arrival in Dar es Salaam — 60,000 TZS (~20€) for two monthly cards with data
  • Yellow fever vaccination required — check all health requirements well in advance
  • Currency: bring USD cash for the safari — everything else you can pay in TZS or card
  • Best time: September–October is perfect — dry, warm and excellent wildlife viewing
What to pack
  • Neutral colours for safari (khaki, beige, olive) — bright colours disturb animals
  • Light layers for cool safari mornings — it gets cold before sunrise
  • Good sunscreen and after-sun — Zanzibar sun is fierce (we bought after-sun there for 20,000 TZS)
  • Insect repellent — essential for both safari and beach
  • Reef-safe sunscreen for snorkelling
  • Comfortable walking sandals for Stone Town's uneven alleys
  • A small amount of USD cash in small bills for tips and markets

Would we go back?

Without a single second of hesitation. Tanzania got under our skin in a way that very few places ever have. There is something about standing on the edge of the Ngorongoro Crater at dawn, or watching the tide go out in Jambiani leaving behind a kilometre of flat sand and silence, that recalibrates everything. Your problems feel smaller. Your gratitude feels bigger.

We spent 17 days and around 5,100€ for two people — including flights from Stuttgart, a full safari, 10 nights on Zanzibar and everything in between. It sounds like a lot. It was worth every euro and then some.
